What is a Steno Tech Assistant (STA)?

An STA acts as the primary technical facilitator for remote depositions, ensuring that all participants can connect and interact without technical friction. Unlike the vidoegrapher role, an STA is typically assigned when a formal video product is not required but professional technical oversight is still necessary.

When You'll Be Assigned as an STA:
  • A video product is NOT ordered (client receives transcript only)
  • The job is on Zoom with the Steno Connect App AND the attorney hasn't completed a Demo OR has requested assistance
  • The job is a hybrid job or has a 3rd party videographer involved

If you're assigned as both REA (Remote Exhibit Assistant) AND STA, you'll stay for the entire deposition. Your responsibilities include showing exhibits and monitoring technical issues throughout, but you do NOT fulfill video job requirements.

üìå Last-Minute REA Protocol: If REA service is added last minute, you MUST message #video-help immediately. In these cases, you must stay for the entire duration of the deposition instead of just the first 15 minutes.

Pre-Job Verification

Before the deposition starts, verify the job type in your Provider Dashboard:
  • Location Field: Check for the label "Hybrid" and a physical conference room address
  • Attendees Section: Look for a "Third-Party Videographer (VID)" listed, which confirms a physical room presence
  • CR Status: Confirm if the Court Reporter (CR) is listed as in-person or remote

2. The 10-Minute Rule (T-10):

  • If the in-person Court Reporter (CR) is missing @ T-10:
    1. Call the CR's phone number directly (from Provider Dashboard)
    2. Leave a voicemail if there is no answer
    3. Message #video-help in Slack

3. Role During Proceeding:

You are the primary technical facilitator responsible for troubleshooting any and all technical issues (including audio, video, and general connectivity) for all participants. You must remain in the room for a 15-minute observation period once the record begins to ensure technical stability.

4. Exit Procedure:

Before leaving the session, you must:

  • Transfer Host: Pass host privileges to the in-person Court Reporter
  • Provide Tech Line: Paste the Tech Support number (888.404.0390) into the meeting chat
Remember the 10-Minute Rule: Always check if in-person participants have connected 10 minutes before the scheduled start time!
